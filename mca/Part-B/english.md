## 1. Parts of Speech

Every word in English belongs to one of these categories:

| Part of Speech   | What it does                                    | Examples               |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| **Noun**         | Names a person, place, thing, idea              | dog, city, love, Ravi  |
| **Pronoun**      | Replaces a noun                                 | he, she, it, they, we  |
| **Verb**         | Action or state of being                        | run, is, think, write  |
| **Adjective**    | Describes a noun                                | big, red, clever, fast |
| **Adverb**       | Describes a verb/adjective/adverb               | quickly, very, well    |
| **Preposition**  | Shows relationship between noun and other words | in, on, at, of, by     |
| **Conjunction**  | Joins words or clauses                          | and, but, or, because  |
| **Interjection** | Expresses sudden emotion                        | Oh!, Wow!, Ouch!       |

## 2. Tenses ⭐

### Tense Structure Table

| Tense                      | Structure                  | Example                   |
| -------------------------- | -------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Simple Present             | V1 / V1+s                  | He writes                 |
| Present Continuous         | is/am/are + V-ing          | He is writing             |
| Present Perfect            | has/have + V3              | He has written            |
| Present Perfect Continuous | has/have + been + V-ing    | He has been writing       |
| Simple Past                | V2                         | He wrote                  |
| Past Continuous            | was/were + V-ing           | He was writing            |
| Past Perfect               | had + V3                   | He had written            |
| Past Perfect Continuous    | had + been + V-ing         | He had been writing       |
| Simple Future              | will/shall + V1            | He will write             |
| Future Continuous          | will + be + V-ing          | He will be writing        |
| Future Perfect             | will + have + V3           | He will have written      |
| Future Perfect Continuous  | will + have + been + V-ing | He will have been writing |

> 🧠 Trick: V1 = base form (write), V2 = past (wrote), V3 = past participle (written), V-ing = present participle (writing)

### Key Usage Rules

- **Simple present** — habitual actions, facts: "The sun rises in the east."
- **Present perfect** — action completed but connected to present: "I have finished my work."
- **Past perfect** — action completed before another past action: "He had left before I arrived."
- **Simple past** — completed action at specific time: "I went to college yesterday."

## 3. Subject-Verb Agreement ⭐

- Singular subject → singular verb
- Plural subject → plural verb

### Important Rules

- **Collective nouns** (team, group, committee) → singular verb: "The team **is** ready."
- **Uncountable nouns** (water, milk, advice, information, furniture, luggage) → singular verb: "The information **is** correct."
- **Either/Neither** (alone) → singular verb: "Neither of them **is** wrong."
- **Either...or / Neither...nor** → verb agrees with the **nearer subject**: "Either he or they **are** wrong."
- **Everyone, everyone, nobody, someone** → singular verb: "Everyone **has** a role."
- **Two subjects joined by AND** → plural verb: "Ram and Shyam **are** friends."
- **Mathematics, Physics, News** → singular verb (look plural but are singular): "The news **is** good."

> 🧠 Trick: Uncountable nouns = always singular. Collective nouns = singular. Everyone/Someone = singular.

## 4. Articles ⭐

Three articles: **a, an, the**

### Rules for "a" and "an"

- **a** — before consonant sounds: a book, a university (u sounds like 'yu'), a one-way
- **an** — before vowel sounds: an apple, an hour (h is silent), an MCA student (M sounds like 'em')

> 🧠 Trick: a/an depends on **sound**, not spelling. "An hour" (h silent → vowel sound). "A university" (sounds like 'yoo').

### Rules for "the"

- Before specific/known noun: "Close **the** door." (a specific door)
- Before unique things: **the** sun, **the** moon, **the** earth
- Before superlatives: **the** best, **the** tallest
- Before ordinals: **the** first, **the** second
- Before rivers, oceans, mountain ranges, newspapers: **the** Ganga, **the** Pacific, **the** Himalayas, **the** Hindu
- Before nationalities used as group: **the** Indians, **the** Americans

### When NOT to use "the"

- Before proper names: Ravi, India, Delhi (NOT the India)
- Before languages: Hindi, English (NOT the Hindi)
- Before meals: breakfast, lunch (NOT the lunch)
- Before sports: cricket, football (NOT the cricket)
- Before Mount, Lake + name: Mount Everest, Lake Victoria

## 5. Prepositions ⭐

Prepositions show relationship between nouns and other words.

### Prepositions of Time

| Preposition    | Use                                         | Example                        |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| **at**         | specific time, clock time                   | at 5pm, at noon, at night      |
| **on**         | days and dates                              | on Monday, on 15th August      |
| **in**         | months, years, seasons, long periods        | in January, in 2026, in summer |
| **since**      | starting point of time (with perfect tense) | since 2020, since Monday       |
| **for**        | duration of time                            | for two hours, for years       |
| **by**         | not later than                              | by Monday, by 5pm              |
| **until/till** | up to a point in time                       | until tomorrow, till midnight  |

### Prepositions of Place

| Preposition | Use                        | Example                    |
| ----------- | -------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| **at**      | specific point             | at the door, at school     |
| **in**      | enclosed space             | in the room, in India      |
| **on**      | surface                    | on the table, on the floor |
| **above**   | higher than, not touching  | the fan above the table    |
| **over**    | directly above OR covering | the bridge over the river  |
| **below**   | lower than                 | below the surface          |
| **under**   | directly below             | under the table            |
| **between** | in the middle of two       | between A and B            |
| **among**   | in the middle of many      | among the students         |

### Common Fixed Prepositions (MCQ favourites)

| Expression               | Correct Preposition |
| ------------------------ | ------------------- |
| Interested **in**        | in                  |
| Guilty **of**            | of                  |
| Accused **of**           | of                  |
| Married **to**           | to                  |
| Addicted **to**          | to                  |
| Depend **on**            | on                  |
| Insist **on**            | on                  |
| Afraid **of**            | of                  |
| Good **at**              | at                  |
| Angry **with** (person)  | with                |
| Angry **at** (situation) | at                  |
| Proud **of**             | of                  |
| Aware **of**             | of                  |

> 🧠 Trick: "Accused of", "Guilty of", "Afraid of", "Proud of", "Aware of" — all use **of**. "Addicted to", "Married to", "Similar to" — all use **to**.

## 6. Active and Passive Voice ⭐

### Structure

- **Active:** Subject + Verb + Object → "Ram **ate** the mango."
- **Passive:** Object + be + V3 + by + Subject → "The mango **was eaten** by Ram."

### Passive Formation Rules

| Tense              | Active     | Passive         |
| ------------------ | ---------- | --------------- |
| Simple Present     | eats       | is/are eaten    |
| Simple Past        | ate        | was/were eaten  |
| Present Continuous | is eating  | is being eaten  |
| Past Continuous    | was eating | was being eaten |
| Present Perfect    | has eaten  | has been eaten  |
| Past Perfect       | had eaten  | had been eaten  |
| Simple Future      | will eat   | will be eaten   |
| Modals             | can eat    | can be eaten    |

> 🧠 Trick: Passive = Object becomes subject + "be" verb in right tense + V3

### Notes

- "By + agent" can be omitted if agent is unknown or obvious
- Intransitive verbs (go, sleep, come) **cannot** be made passive

## 7. Direct and Indirect Speech ⭐

### Reporting Verbs

- Direct: He said, "I am happy."
- Indirect: He said that he **was** happy.

### Rules for Tense Change

| Direct    | Indirect |
| --------- | -------- |
| is/am/are | was/were |
| was/were  | had been |
| has/have  | had      |
| will      | would    |
| can       | could    |
| may       | might    |
| shall     | would    |

### Pronoun Changes

- "I" → he/she (based on speaker)
- "we" → they
- "you" → I/he/she/they (based on context)
- "my" → his/her

### Time/Place Word Changes

| Direct    | Indirect         |
| --------- | ---------------- |
| now       | then             |
| today     | that day         |
| yesterday | the previous day |
| tomorrow  | the next day     |
| here      | there            |
| this      | that             |
| these     | those            |

## 8. Modals ⭐

| Modal        | Use                                 | Example                     |
| ------------ | ----------------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| **can**      | ability, informal permission        | I can swim                  |
| **could**    | past ability, polite request        | Could you help me?          |
| **may**      | formal permission, possibility      | It may rain                 |
| **might**    | less certain possibility            | He might come               |
| **will**     | future, strong intention            | I will go                   |
| **would**    | polite request, conditional         | Would you like tea?         |
| **shall**    | future (I/we), suggestion           | Shall we go?                |
| **should**   | advice, moral obligation            | You should study            |
| **must**     | strong necessity, certainty         | You must wear seatbelt      |
| **ought to** | moral obligation (weaker than must) | You ought to respect elders |
| **need not** | no necessity                        | You need not come           |
| **dare**     | challenge, courage                  | How dare you!               |

> 🧠 Trick: must > should > ought to (decreasing obligation). may > might (decreasing possibility).

## 14. Reading Comprehension — Strategy ⭐

### Approach

1. **Skim the passage first** — get the main idea (30 seconds)
2. **Read the questions** before reading in detail
3. **Read carefully** — focus on what questions ask about
4. **Answer from the passage** — do not use outside knowledge
5. For "tone" questions — look for adjectives and strong language
6. For "title" questions — find the central idea of the whole passage
7. For "inference" questions — the answer is implied, not stated directly

### Question Types

- **Main idea** — what is the passage mostly about?
- **Specific detail** — find a fact directly stated in the passage
- **Inference** — what can be concluded from the passage?
- **Vocabulary in context** — what does this word mean as used here?
- **Author's tone** — what is the attitude of the writer? (critical, neutral, appreciative...)
- **Title** — best heading for the passage

> 🧠 Trick: The answer to comprehension questions is ALWAYS in the passage. Never guess from general knowledge.

## 15. Technical Writing (general)

- **Technical writing** — clear, precise, audience-focused writing about technical topics
- Key qualities: clarity, conciseness, accuracy, objectivity
- Examples: manuals, reports, documentation, emails

### Features of Good Technical Writing

- Use simple, direct language
- Avoid jargon unless necessary (define if used)
- Use active voice where possible
- Short sentences preferred
- Use headings, bullet points for structure
- Audience awareness — write for who will read it

## 17. Key Facts for MCQs ⭐

- "a" before consonant sound, "an" before vowel sound — based on SOUND not spelling
- "an hour" — h is silent → vowel sound → "an"
- "a university" — u sounds like 'yu' → consonant sound → "a"
- Fewer = countable | Less = uncountable
- Between = 2 items | Among = 3+ items
- Affect = verb | Effect = noun
- Advice = noun | Advise = verb
- Uncountable nouns always take singular verb (information, furniture, luggage)
- Everyone/Someone/Nobody = singular verb
- "Either...or / Neither...nor" → verb agrees with nearest subject
- Active → Passive: object becomes subject + be + V3
- Passive not possible for intransitive verbs (go, come, sleep)
- In indirect speech: will → would, can → could, may → might
- In indirect speech: "now" → "then", "today" → "that day"
- Omnivore = eats everything | Herbivore = plants only | Carnivore = meat only
- Omni = all (omniscient, omnipotent, omnipresent)
- Comprehension answers are ALWAYS from the passage, not outside knowledge
- Hydrophobia = fear of water | Claustrophobia = fear of closed spaces | Acrophobia = fear of heights

## 18. Practice MCQs

1. Choose correct article: He is \_\_\_ honest man. → **an** (h is silent, vowel sound)
2. Choose correct article: She is \_\_\_ MBA student. → **an** (M sounds like 'em')
3. Fill preposition: He is good \_\_\_ mathematics. → **at**
4. Fill preposition: She is married \_\_\_ a doctor. → **to**
5. Fill preposition: He was accused \_\_\_ theft. → **of**
6. Fewer or less: There are \_\_\_ students today. → **fewer** (students = countable)
7. Fewer or less: There is \_\_\_ water in the glass. → **less** (water = uncountable)
8. Active to passive: "Ram wrote the letter." → **The letter was written by Ram.**
9. Subject-verb: The news \_\_\_ (is/are) shocking. → **is** (news = singular)
10. Subject-verb: Everyone \_\_\_ (has/have) a ticket. → **has** (everyone = singular)
11. Synonym of "Diligent" → **Hardworking / Industrious**
12. Antonym of "Arrogant" → **Humble**
13. One word: One who cannot read or write → **Illiterate**
14. One word: Fear of water → **Hydrophobia**
15. Idiom "Under the weather" means → **Feeling sick/unwell**
16. Idiom "Once in a blue moon" means → **Very rarely**
17. Correct spelling: Recieve or Receive? → **Receive**
18. Correct spelling: Seperate or Separate? → **Separate**
19. Tense: She \_\_\_ (live/has lived) here since 2010. → **has lived** (since = present perfect)
20. Indirect speech: He said, "I am hungry." → **He said that he was hungry.**
21. Between or Among: Divide this \_\_\_ the five students. → **among** (more than two)
22. Affect or Effect: The cold weather \_\_\_ his health. → **affected** (verb)
23. Modal for advice: You \_\_\_ see a doctor. → **should**
24. Modal for strong necessity: You \_\_\_ wear a helmet. → **must**
25. Comprehension strategy: Answer should come from? → **The passage only**
